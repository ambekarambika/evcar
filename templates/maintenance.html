EV Maintenance Guidelines

Electric vehicles require less maintenance than internal combustion engines, but regular upkeep is still essential for peak efficiency, safety, and lifespan.

Learn More

Battery Care

Your EV's battery pack is its most critical component. Ensuring proper state of charge limits preserves its active life.

  • Avoid deep discharge (do not let the battery drop below 20%).
  • Maintain optimal charge (keep it charged between 20% and 80% for daily usage).

Tire Care

EVs are heavier due to battery weight and deliver instant torque, leading to faster tire wear if neglected.

  • Check tire pressure monthly to maximize range and handling.
  • Rotate tires every 10,000 km to ensure symmetrical tread wear.

Brake Maintenance

Regenerative braking does most of the stopping work, reducing physical brake wear, but friction brakes still need checkups.

  • Inspect mechanical brake pads, rotors, and calipers every 20,000 km to prevent seizing.

Software Updates

EVs rely heavily on operating systems to manage thermal controls, battery efficiency, and motor performance.

  • Install updates regularly to access battery management enhancements and safety fixes.
