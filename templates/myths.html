EV Myths vs Facts

Debunk common misconceptions about electric vehicles and get the facts backed by research and real-world statistics.

Myth EVs are slow and lack driving performance
Fact
EVs have instant torque. Unlike internal combustion engines that must rev up to deliver power, electric motors provide maximum torque from 0 RPM. This allows for rapid, quiet, and extremely smooth acceleration.
Myth EV batteries degrade and die within a few years
Fact
Most last 8-15 years. Battery management systems (BMS) manage charging limits and cooling. Most EV manufacturers offer warranties on the battery pack for 8 years or 160,000 km, guaranteeing at least 70-80% capacity retention.
Myth EVs are unsafe to drive and represent fire hazards
Fact
EVs undergo strict safety testing. Electric vehicles must pass the exact same crash safety testing standards as regular cars. In addition, battery enclosures are heavily protected to prevent thermal runaway, and EV models routinely earn 5-star safety scores from global crash assessors.
