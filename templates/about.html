About Electric Vehicles

Electric Vehicles (EVs) are automobiles powered by electric motors using energy stored in rechargeable batteries.

Battery Pack

Stores electrical energy for the vehicle. This is charged from the electricity grid and provides direct current (DC) power.

Controller

Controls power delivery from the battery. It regulates voltage and manages the speed of the electric motor depending on throttle input.

Electric Motor

Converts electrical energy into mechanical movement, providing torque and turning the wheels.

Types of EVs

Battery Electric Vehicle (BEV)

Runs entirely on a battery-powered electric drivetrain, storing electricity in a pack charged from external grid ports.

  • Characteristics: No fuel tank, Fully electric
  • Examples: Tata Nexon EV, Tesla Model 3

Plug-in Hybrid EV (PHEV)

Combines a combustion engine with an electric motor. The battery pack can be charged externally and runs in electric-first mode.

  • Characteristics: Electric + Petrol Engine
  • Examples: Toyota Prius Prime

Hybrid Electric Vehicle (HEV)

Dual power sources that split work automatically. The battery cannot be plugged in and is charged solely on the go.

  • Characteristics: Regenerative charging
  • Examples: Toyota Camry Hybrid

Benefits of Electric Vehicles

Environmental Benefits

  • Reduced greenhouse gases
  • Cleaner air due to zero tailpipe emissions

Economic Benefits

  • Lower fuel expenses (electricity vs petrol)
  • Government subsidies and tax credits

Performance Benefits

  • Faster acceleration with instant torque
  • Quiet and comfortable cabin operation
